# Book Lessons

## Purpose

Book Lessons allows users to enter any book title and receive a structured lesson based on the book's core ideas.

The goal is to turn books into guided learning experiences without reproducing copyrighted material.

All content must be written in original language that summarizes, interprets, and explains the book's ideas.

---

# Lesson Structure

Every Book Lesson must include the following sections in order.

## Book Overview

A clear explanation of what the book is about.
Include the author's core argument and why the book matters.
Keep this to 2–4 sentences.

## Key Principles

5–10 major ideas, frameworks, or lessons from the book.
Each principle should be explained clearly with practical context.
Write each principle as a titled insight.

## Practical Takeaways

3–5 actionable insights the user can apply immediately.
Focus on behavior change, not just information.

## Who This Book Helps Most

Describe the type of reader who benefits most from this book.
Be specific about the situation, challenge, or goal the book addresses.

## Potential Critiques

Provide a balanced perspective.
Note common criticisms or limitations of the book's ideas.
This helps the user think critically rather than accept ideas unchecked.

---

# Lesson Modes

## Quick

Short and focused.
Cover the Book Overview and the top 3–5 Key Principles only.
Keep the total response under 300 words.
Ideal for users who want a fast mental summary.

## Deep Dive

Detailed and expansive.
Cover all sections fully: Overview, Key Principles, Takeaways, Who It Helps, Critiques.
Explain each principle in depth with examples where relevant.
Ideal for users who want to fully understand the book.

## Interactive

Teach step by step.
Present one idea at a time.
After each section, include a reflection question the user can think about.
End with a challenge the user can take into their day.
Ideal for users who want active engagement with the material.

---

# System Rules

The system must follow these rules when generating Book Lessons.

- Do not reproduce copyrighted text from the book.
- Write all content in original language.
- Summarize, interpret, and explain — do not quote at length.
- Keep the tone clear, practical, and grounded.
- Focus on how the book's ideas apply to real decisions and personal growth.
- Stay consistent with the My Tribe philosophy: the user is responsible for their decisions.

---

# Continue with Tribe

After a Book Lesson is generated, the user may continue by selecting an interaction mode:

Ask a Member → advisor gives perspective on the book's ideas
Ask Your Guide → guide reflects on the book through their lens
Ask BVM → best version of you applies the book's top lesson
Ask My Tribe → full tribe responds to the book's core message
Show Parable → an advisor illustrates a book idea through story

The book title should be included as context when the user continues the conversation.
