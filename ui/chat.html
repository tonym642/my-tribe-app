Chat Guide

Ask a Member
Speak directly with one advisor from your Tribe. Each member has their own personality, background, and perspective. Choose someone whose lens fits your question.
Ask My Tribe
Send your question to the entire council at once. Each advisor replies in turn, giving you a multi-perspective response in a single conversation thread.
Ask BVM
BVM stands for Best Version of Me — an advisor built from your own values and goals. Ask BVM when you want to hear what your best self would say.
Ask Your Guide
Your Guide is a personal mentor you define yourself — a Stoic philosopher, your future self, a trusted elder. Configure your Guide's identity in Settings.

Sessions Guide

Debate
Pose a question or dilemma and watch your Tribe argue it from all sides. Each advisor defends their position, then a closing summary is generated. Best for decisions where you want to see every angle challenged.
Voting
Give the council a choice and ask them to vote. Each advisor casts their vote and explains their reasoning. The result shows a tally, the winning option, and a guide summary. Best for binary or multi-option decisions.
Campfire
Gather the Tribe for a reflective circle. Share a topic, set the tone — Reflective, Celebratory, or Healing — and each advisor offers wisdom in the spirit of that tone. Best for processing emotions or marking a meaningful moment.

Stories Guide

Tribe Stories are short parables that each advisor generates in response to your question. Instead of advice, they offer a story — a metaphor drawn from their world and perspective.
How they work
When you ask a question in Ask My Tribe mode with parables enabled, each advisor writes a brief story rooted in their background. The story carries a lesson that applies to your situation indirectly, the way a good parable does.
Reading stories
Open the Stories panel from the navigation menu. You'll find all stories from today's session there, organized by advisor. Each story shows its title, type, and the lesson it carries.
Archiving
Stories are marked as new when generated. Once read, they move to the archive. You can switch between new and archived stories from the Stories panel header.
