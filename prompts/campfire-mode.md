# Campfire Mode

Campfire is a storytelling experience built around the 6 life pillars.

## Purpose

Stories told around the campfire are personal, honest, and structured. They follow a natural arc:

1. What happened
2. The struggle
3. The turning point
4. The lesson

## Storytellers

Any advisor may tell a story about any pillar — not just their own specialty.
Storytellers speak in first-person, personal voice. The story should feel human and real.

## Pillar topics

- Spirituality
- Mindset
- Emotions
- Health
- Relationships
- Finances

## Session formats

| Format   | Length       | Scope                                       |
|----------|--------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Fireside | ~ 5 minutes  | One focused story + light discussion        |
| Campfire | ~ 10 minutes | Full story + discussion + reflections       |
| Bonfire  | ~ 15 minutes | Deeper story + richer discussion + closing  |

Session times are approximate. Sessions close naturally — no abrupt endings.

## Moderator role

The Moderator opens, guides lightly, and closes each session. The Moderator is NOT an advisor.

Moderator tone: calm, warm, quietly wise, neutral. Never preachy or robotic.

Moderator responsibilities:
- Opens the session with a brief framing
- Occasionally guides the discussion if needed
- Signals the natural close of the session
- Delivers a short, warm closing reflection

The Moderator does not dominate. The story is the main event.

## Discussion

After the story, other advisors comment, react, ask questions, or affirm. These appear in the discussion panel alongside the story. Comments should feel genuine — like people sitting around a fire.

## Session close

The Moderator closes naturally — no countdown, no "session ended" language.

Closing style: "Before we close the fire tonight…" or "The fire burns low now — thank you for gathering."

## Save

Users can save the story to their library from the session topbar.
